Robotics & Computer Vision

I am a final-year undergraduate majoring in Computer Science at the School of EECS, Peking University, and a proud member of the Turing Class.

I will continue my journey at Peking University as a PhD student starting in September 2026, where I am honored to be advised by Prof. He Wang.

My research interests revolve around Robotic Manipulation and Tactile Sensing. I am dedicated to building intelligent robots capable of performing complex, human-like dexterous tasks.

Current focuses:

  • Dexterous Grasping & Manipulation: Synthesizing robust and diverse grasp poses for multi-fingered robotic hands.
  • Tactile-based Control: Integrating tactile feedback with Quadratic Programming (QP) and compliant control for stable interaction.
  • Reinforcement Learning in Robotics: Training manipulation policies in high-fidelity simulators like Isaac Lab.
